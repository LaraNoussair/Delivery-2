Unique landscapes

A 10 000 year-old legacy

In Brazil, sand dunes and lagoons blend to form the Lençóis Maranhenses National Park, literally the "Sheets of Maranhão," a state in the Northeast of the country. The origin of this surprising landscape? Sediments from the Parnaíba River, pushed by tides towards the coast for over 10,000 years. When they encounter the first barrier, they accumulate and form dunes.

Within the 1,500 km² of the park, several hundred inhabitants primarily make a living from goat or pig farming, fishing, and more recently, tourism..

Walking through the dunes, you will encounter the astonishing silence of the desert and the gentle melodies of animals in the oases.
